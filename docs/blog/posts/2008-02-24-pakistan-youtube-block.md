# Pakistan YouTube Block

## Summary
In February 2008, the Pakistani government blocked access to YouTube nationwide following the upload of controversial content deemed blasphemous, leading to a prolonged internet censorship event.

## Timeline
- **February 24, 2008**: Pakistan Telecom issues blocking order after videos insulting Islam are uploaded.
- **Immediate**: YouTube becomes inaccessible across Pakistan.
- **March 2008**: YouTube removes the videos, but block remains.
- **2009-2010**: Block partially lifted, but YouTube remains restricted.
- **2016**: Full unblock after negotiations.

## Root Cause
- Upload of anti-Islam videos by Dutch politician Geert Wilders.
- Pakistani government response to protect religious sentiments.
- Technical implementation via BGP route hijacking by Pakistan Telecom.

## Impact
- Complete blockage of YouTube for Pakistani users.
- Affected millions of users, businesses, and education.
- Led to global routing issues and collateral blocking in other countries.

## Resolution
- YouTube complied by geo-blocking the videos in Pakistan.
- Gradual lifting of restrictions over years.

## Lessons Learned
- Internet censorship can have far-reaching effects.
- Importance of international cooperation on content moderation.
- BGP security vulnerabilities highlighted.
